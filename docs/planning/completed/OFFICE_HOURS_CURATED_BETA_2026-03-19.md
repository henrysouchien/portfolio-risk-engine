## Problem Statement

Self-directed investors who manage their own money across multiple brokers have no affordable way to get personalized, institutional-quality portfolio intelligence. The problem isn't connecting brokers or getting data — it's getting **actionable insights** about YOUR specific portfolio: What's my real risk? How am I actually performing? What should I change?

This is especially acute for ex-finance professionals who once had Bloomberg terminals and institutional research infrastructure, and now manage personal portfolios with nothing but broker dashboards and gut feel. Their investing quality collapses without the analytical infrastructure they're accustomed to.

## Demand Evidence

- **Founder is user #1**: Built this system to solve his own problem after leaving equity research. The pain is real and lived — investing quality "fell off a cliff" without institutional tools.
- **Risk score interest**: People from the investing world expressed specific interest in the risk score feature — wanting to know "what would it say about THEIR portfolio." This is early but directional.
- **Gap in market**: No existing product combines multi-broker aggregation + institutional-grade risk analysis (factor exposure, stress testing, VaR, realized performance) + AI agent that can reason about all of it. This combination is genuinely novel.
- **Unvalidated at scale**: No paying customers, no structured user research. The founder has been heads-down building rather than validating externally.

## Status Quo

Before building risk_module, the founder's workflow was:
1. **At the firm**: Bloomberg terminal + IBKR — institutional data, tools, and workflows. Close to markets daily.
2. **After leaving**: Attempted Google Sheets / Excel for tracking. Always out of date. Manual, tedious, and fragile.
3. **Result**: Investing quality degraded. Stopped doing systematic risk management. Relied on intuition.

The status quo for the target user today: log into each broker separately, maybe pull holdings into a spreadsheet, make decisions without systematic risk analysis. The alternative to this product isn't a competitor — it's doing nothing and investing worse.

## Target User & Narrowest Wedge

**Target user (hypothesis — needs validation):**
Self-directed investors with $100K+ across multiple accounts who are sophisticated enough to care about risk but don't have institutional tools. Two sub-segments to test:
1. **Ex-finance professionals** managing personal money (know what good analysis looks like, miss it)
2. **Sophisticated retail investors** who've outgrown Robinhood/Schwab dashboards (active options traders, multi-broker)

**Narrowest wedge:**
"Upload your portfolio. Get an AI-generated risk analysis with a risk score, factor exposures, stress test results, and 3 specific recommendations — in 60 seconds."

The risk score is the hook (simple, shareable, provocative). The depth behind it (factor analysis, stress testing, realized performance) is what makes it credible and retainable.

## Constraints

- Founder is solo — no team, limited time for user research alongside deploy work
- Product spans 3 repos: risk_module (engine), ai-excel-addin (agent surface), investment_tools (supporting)
- Currently focused on getting the existing system deployed — UI/UX polish, not new features
- No pricing model established
- Broker connections (IBKR, Schwab, Plaid) work but require setup — friction for onboarding
- IBKR Flex query has a ~12-month historical data window (hard constraint)

## Premises

All agreed by founder:

1. **The value is the insight, not the infrastructure.** People don't care about broker connections or data aggregation. They want personalized, AI-driven answers about THEIR portfolio — risk score, what-if scenarios, specific recommendations. The plumbing is invisible; the opinion is the product.

2. **The engine depth is the moat.** "We have AI" is not a moat. 16+ specialized MCP tools, 3000+ tests, and deep domain logic (factor analysis, stress testing, option pricing, realized performance) is. The agent's answers are only as good as the analytical engine behind them.

3. **Discovery is the priority, not more engineering.** The technical foundation is proven. The existential risk is building in isolation without validating that other people will pay for AI-powered portfolio intelligence. The next phase is finding 5-10 people who need this, not shipping more features.

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: Risk Score Lead Magnet (Narrowest Wedge)
- Landing page where anyone pastes holdings → instant risk score + 3-sentence AI analysis
- Effort: S (human: ~1 week / CC: ~2 hours)
- Risk: Low
- Pros: Fastest to launch, zero friction, tests atomic value prop, generates email list
- Cons: Doesn't test willingness to pay, doesn't show full capability, may attract tire-kickers
- Completeness: 4/10

### Approach B: Curated Beta — 10 Hand-Picked Users
- Deploy full agent + dashboard, hand-pick 10 users, 1:1 onboarding, observe usage
- Effort: M (human: ~2 weeks / CC: ~4 hours)
- Risk: Medium
- Pros: Tests real product, reveals what matters, observation > surveys, builds champion relationships
- Cons: Slower, requires recruitment, small sample, selection bias
- Completeness: 8/10

### Approach C: Content-Led — Public Portfolio Analysis
- Publicly analyze famous portfolios (Buffett 13F, ARK, popular ETFs) using the agent
- Effort: S (human: ~3 days / CC: ~1 hour)
- Risk: Low
- Pros: Demonstrates intelligence publicly, shareable content, attracts right audience
- Cons: Doesn't test personal portfolio use case
- Completeness: 5/10

## Recommended Approach

**Approach B: Curated Beta** — chosen because it tests the real product with real users and produces observation-based evidence, not interest-based guesses. The founder is already working on deployment — this approach adds intentional user selection and structured observation to the deploy work already in progress.

Optional enhancement: run Approach C in parallel as a content flywheel to attract beta candidates.

## Open Questions

1. **Who are the 10?** The founder needs to identify 10 specific people to invite. Ex-colleagues from equity research are the natural starting point. Investing community contacts who expressed interest in the risk score are candidates.
2. **What does "onboarding" look like?** Is it a Zoom call where you watch them? An async setup with Loom videos? The observation method matters.
3. **What's the pricing hypothesis?** Even if the beta is free, having a price in mind ($X/month) helps frame the value proposition and sets up the "would you pay for this?" conversation.
4. **How do users get their portfolio in?** Manual upload (CSV/paste)? Broker connection (Plaid/IBKR)? The onboarding friction will determine whether people actually complete setup.
5. **Which repo is the user-facing surface?** ai-excel-addin (Excel agent) or risk_module (web dashboard)? Or both? This affects what you deploy for beta.
6. **Multi-repo deploy story**: How do the 3 repos work together in production? Is there a unified deploy, or do users interact with each separately?

## Success Criteria

- [ ] 10 beta users onboarded and actively using the product within 4 weeks of deploy
- [ ] At least 3 users return unprompted after initial session (retention signal)
- [ ] At least 1 user describes the value in their own words differently than you expected (surprise signal)
- [ ] Identify which features users actually use vs. which you assumed they'd use
- [ ] Have a specific answer to "would you pay $X/month for this?" from at least 5 users
- [ ] At least 1 user tells someone else about it without being asked (organic referral signal)

## Dependencies

- **Deploy**: The product needs to be deployed and accessible. This is in progress (UI/UX polish).
- **Auth + multi-tenancy**: The system needs to support multiple users with isolated portfolios. Verify this works.
- **Beta user recruitment**: Founder needs to reach out to investing contacts and ex-colleagues. This is the #1 action item.
- **Observation protocol**: Define how you'll watch users — screen share, session recording, async feedback? Choose before first onboarding.
