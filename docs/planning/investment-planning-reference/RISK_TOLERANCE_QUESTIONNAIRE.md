# Investment Goals & Risk Tolerance Questionnaire

Source: Google Sheet `Investment-Goals-Risk-Tolerance-Questionnaire`

## Risk Tolerance Assessment (10 Questions, scored 1-4)

### 1. Income Risk
How would you rate the outlook of your employment, income, or business?
- (4) Very positive — high income growth potential, diversified income streams
- (3) Positive — stable income with moderate growth
- (2) Moderate — some uncertainty or limited growth
- (1) Negative — job instability or income decline

### 2. Health Risk
How would you rate your current health condition?
- (4) Excellent
- (3) Good
- (2) Fair
- (1) Poor

### 3. Risk Tolerance
If the market went down 20% this year, how much of your investment portfolio are you willing to lose?
- (4) 20% or more — I'd buy more
- (3) 10-20% — I'd hold
- (2) 5-10% — I'd be uncomfortable
- (1) Less than 5% — I'd sell

### 4. Return Expectations
If the market went up 20% this year, how much do you expect your portfolio to gain?
- (4) 20% or more
- (3) 10-20%
- (2) 5-10%
- (1) Less than 5%

### 5. Cash Flow Risk
What are your expected cash needs in the next 5 years?
- (4) No major cash needs expected
- (3) Some moderate expenses planned
- (2) Significant expenses coming (house, education)
- (1) Large cash needs imminent

### 6. Available Capital
What is your current savings rate (% of income saved)?
- (4) Over 20%
- (3) 10-20%
- (2) 5-10%
- (1) Under 5%

### 7. Financial Risk
What is your net worth to total assets ratio?
- (4) Over 0.75 (strong equity position)
- (3) 0.50-0.75
- (2) 0.25-0.50
- (1) Under 0.25 (high leverage)

### 8. Liquidity Risk
How many months of cash reserves do you have?
- (4) 12+ months
- (3) 6-12 months
- (2) 3-6 months
- (1) Less than 3 months

### 9. Operating Risk
What is your expense outlook?
- (4) Expenses declining or very stable
- (3) Expenses stable
- (2) Expenses rising moderately
- (1) Expenses rising significantly

### 10. Expense Risk
What is your living expense to income ratio?
- (4) Under 40%
- (3) 40-55%
- (2) 55-70%
- (1) Over 70%

## Scoring

| Total Score | Risk Profile | Investment Approach |
|-------------|-------------|-------------------|
| 35-40 | Aggressive | Growth-oriented, higher equity allocation |
| 25-34 | Moderate-Aggressive | Balanced with growth tilt |
| 15-24 | Moderate | Balanced stocks/bonds |
| 10-14 | Conservative | Income-focused, capital preservation |

## Goal Definition

For each investment goal, define:
1. **Goal name** — What are you investing for? (retirement, house, education, emergency fund, etc.)
2. **Timeframe** — When do you need this money? (years)
3. **Return target** — What annual return do you need to reach your goal?
4. **Asset types** — What types of investments are appropriate for this goal's timeframe and risk level?
